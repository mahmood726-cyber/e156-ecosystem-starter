Randomise, follow up, analyse

A trial's credibility lives in its flow: who was randomised, who was lost, who was analysed. CONSORT makes that accounting explicit; the primary outcome then turns two event counts into a relative and an absolute effect.

What this means — in plain language

This is one trial laid bare: how many people were randomised and analysed, the effect on the main outcome in several forms, and whether the trial was ever big enough to detect it.

Assurance

    
Same self-auditing trust layer: the CONSORT flow is derived from leaf counts and turns red if a box goes negative or the allocated arms don't sum to the randomised total, the primary outcome reports both relative (RR/OR) and absolute (ARR/NNT) effects, and achieved N is checked against the N the assumed effect requires. Accent is seeded from this capsule's slug. Tier reaches Gold only on independent reproduction.

How to read this · limitations. A CONSORT flow diagram should reconcile at every stage — enrolled, randomised, analysed — and any discrepancy signals attrition or reporting gaps that can bias the result. Intention-to-treat keeps all randomised participants in their original arm. Demo data are illustrative.
