Genes as a natural experiment

Inherited variants are allocated at conception, before disease — so genetic proxies for an exposure can estimate its causal effect, free of reverse causation. Three estimators that fail in different ways must agree before that claim holds.

Causal · MR — living · live re-run illustrative demo data

What this means — in plain language

Using gene variants randomly dealt at conception as a natural experiment, this estimates whether an exposure truly causes an outcome, sidestepping the confounding that clouds ordinary observational studies.

Protocol

DesignTwo-sample summary-data Mendelian randomization. InstrumentsIndependent SNPs associated with the exposure (βX) and their effect on the outcome (βY). 02

Instruments

SNP associations are illustrative and editable in stage 4; all three estimators re-run as you type. Use genome-wide-significant, independent (LD-clumped) SNPs.

03

Assumptions

MR rests on three assumptions: the SNPs (i) associate with the exposure (relevance), (ii) share no confounder with the outcome (independence), and (iii) affect the outcome only through the exposure (exclusion / no pleiotropy). The Egger intercept probes the third.

06

Sensitivity

—

MR is credible when IVW, MR-Egger and the weighted median broadly agree and the Egger intercept is near zero. A non-zero intercept signals directional pleiotropy — the exclusion assumption is breached and IVW is biased. Weak instruments (small βX/seX) bias estimates toward the confounded observational association.

How to read this · limitations. Mendelian randomization is only as good as its instruments: each must be strongly associated with the exposure, independent of confounders, and affect the outcome only through the exposure (no pleiotropy). IVW assumes no directional pleiotropy — compare with MR-Egger / weighted-median before trusting it. Demo data are illustrative.
