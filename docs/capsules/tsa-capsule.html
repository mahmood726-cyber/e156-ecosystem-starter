Is the meta-analysis large enough to trust?

Repeatedly testing a growing meta-analysis inflates the false-positive rate. TSA borrows a clinical trial's defence: a required information size and monitoring boundaries that the cumulative evidence must cross before "significant" means conclusive.

Type 11 · TSA — living · live re-run illustrative demo data

What this means — in plain language

Just as peeking at a trial repeatedly inflates false alarms, so does updating a meta-analysis with each new study. This draws a monitoring boundary the cumulative evidence must cross, and shows how much information is still needed.

cumulative Z vs information

Green curve = cumulative Z (oriented so benefit is upward). The flaring purple curves are the O'Brien–Fleming boundaries (z = zα/2/√t); grey dashes are the naïve ±zα/2. The amber vertical is the heterogeneity-adjusted RIS. Crossing the boundary before the RIS = conclusive; touching naïve significance without crossing = not yet.
