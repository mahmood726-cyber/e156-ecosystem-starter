Will the trial result hold in our patients?

A trial's effect transports to a new population only if that population looks similar on the things that modify the effect. When it doesn't, re-standardising the trial's subgroup effects to the target's case-mix shows where the effect really lands.

HTA · transportability — living · live re-run illustrative demo data

What this means — in plain language

A trial's result may not carry to a different population if the people who respond differently are present in different proportions. This re-weights the trial effect to a target population.

03

Assumptions

Transportability assumes all effect modifiers are measured and the subgroup effects themselves transport (only their prevalence differs). It cannot fix differences on unmeasured modifiers — like the E-value caveat for confounding, this is an untestable assumption.

How to read this · limitations. Transporting a trial's effect to a target population assumes the trial measured every effect-modifier that differs between the two and that the effect is constant within strata; large standardised differences on key modifiers warn that the result may not carry over.
