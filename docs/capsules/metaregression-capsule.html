Does the effect depend on something?

A pooled estimate assumes one true effect; meta-regression asks whether it slides with a study-level moderator — baseline risk, dose, year — and reports how much of the heterogeneity that moderator explains.

Type 8 · Meta-regression — living · live re-run illustrative demo data

What this means — in plain language

This asks whether a study-level factor — baseline risk, dose, year — explains why trials disagree, by fitting a line through their effects and seeing how much of the scatter it accounts for.

04

Extraction — editable

OR + 95% CI + moderator

Each trial contributes an OR with its 95% CI (pooled on the log scale) and a moderator value. The model is a random-effects meta-regression: yᵢ = β₀ + β₁·xᵢ + uᵢ + εᵢ, with residual between-study variance τ²_res estimated by method of moments. Edit, add or remove trials.

A high R² means the moderator accounts for much of the between-study variance — but with few trials this is fragile and prone to ecological bias (a study-level association need not hold within patients). Cochrane advises ≥10 studies per moderator; the ribbon flags when you have fewer.

08
Assurance

    
Same self-auditing trust layer: pooling and regression are on the log scale and back-transformed, residual τ² and R² are always shown beside the slope (a slope without them invites over-interpretation), and the ribbon warns when fewer than 10 trials support the moderator. Accent is seeded from this capsule's slug. Tier reaches Gold only on independent reproduction.

How to read this · limitations. A non-significant moderator slope does not prove there is no effect-modification — power is low with few studies. Beware aggregation bias: a study-level association can differ from the patient-level one. Demo data is illustrative.
