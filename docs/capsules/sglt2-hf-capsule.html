Sources & strategy

Three sources are searched and de-duplicated: PubMed/MEDLINE, ClinicalTrials.gov (via the AACT mirror), and OpenAlex. The exact query for each is below — copyable and re-runnable. yields illustrative; the queries are real

1 · PubMed / MEDLINE

("Sodium-Glucose Transporter 2 Inhibitors"[Mesh] OR "SGLT2 inhibitor*"[tiab]
   OR dapagliflozin[tiab] OR empagliflozin[tiab] OR sotagliflozin[tiab]
   OR canagliflozin[tiab] OR ertugliflozin[tiab])
AND ("Heart Failure"[Mesh] OR "heart failure"[tiab] OR "cardiac failure"[tiab])
AND (randomized controlled trial[pt] OR randomized[tiab] OR randomised[tiab]
   OR placebo[tiab] OR "controlled trial"[tiab])
Filters: Humans

2 · ClinicalTrials.gov — AACT (PostgreSQL mirror)

SELECT DISTINCT s.nct_id, s.brief_title, s.overall_status
FROM studies s
JOIN interventions i      ON i.nct_id = s.nct_id
JOIN browse_conditions bc ON bc.nct_id = s.nct_id
WHERE s.study_type = 'INTERVENTIONAL'
  AND lower(i.name) ~ '(dapagliflozin|empagliflozin|sotagliflozin|canagliflozin|ertugliflozin|sglt2)'
  AND lower(bc.mesh_term) LIKE '%heart failure%'
  AND s.overall_status = 'COMPLETED';
-- intervention names are matched lowercase (AACT stores mixed case);
-- verify column names against your AACT snapshot before running.

06

Methods

PRISMA #5–15 · narrative

Protocol and registration

This synthesis followed a pre-specified living protocol (v1.0) and is reported in line with PRISMA 2020. The review question, eligibility criteria, outcome and analysis plan were fixed before data extraction; the capsule re-runs the planned analysis whenever the underlying data change.

Eligibility criteria

We included randomised, placebo-controlled outcome trials of an SGLT2 inhibitor added to guideline-directed therapy in adults with heart failure across the full ejection-fraction spectrum, reporting a hazard ratio for the composite of cardiovascular death or worsening heart failure. Trials whose population was type 2 diabetes or chronic kidney disease without heart failure as the index condition, non-randomised studies, and secondary/subgroup publications of already-included trials were excluded (see Screening for each excluded record and its reason).

Information sources and search

ClinicalTrials.gov and PubMed were searched and the results de-duplicated by NCT identifier and then by normalised title. The search log shown is illustrative; the included set is fixed and every record is listed with a link to its registry entry and publication.

Study selection and data extraction

Records were screened by title/abstract and then full text against the eligibility criteria. For each included trial we extracted the 2×2 event counts (events and totals in each arm), the reported hazard ratio and its 95% confidence interval, the agent, year and risk-of-bias domains — each value traceable to the trial's registry record and primary publication (see Extraction). A live denominator check confirms events never exceed the arm total.

Risk of bias

Each trial was appraised with the Cochrane RoB 2 tool across its five domains; the per-domain judgements and an algorithmically-derived overall rating are shown in the Risk-of-bias matrix (ratings illustrative pending adjudication).

Effect measures and synthesis

Hazard ratios were pooled on the natural-log scale by inverse-variance random-effects meta-analysis. Each trial's log-HR standard error is recovered from its confidence interval as (ln UCL − ln LCL)/(2·1.96). The between-study variance τ² is estimated by Paule–Mandel by default (DerSimonian–Laird is biased for k<10; REML and DL are selectable). A 95% prediction interval is computed on tk−1 degrees of freedom (Cochrane Handbook v6.5). An optional Hartung–Knapp–Sidik–Jonkman adjustment is available for the confidence interval.

Heterogeneity, robustness and reporting bias

Heterogeneity is summarised by I² and τ². Robustness is probed by leave-one-out influence, a GOSH plot of every subset meta-analysis, a cumulative meta-analysis by year, subgroup analysis by agent, and meta-regression on trial year. Small-study/publication-bias effects are assessed by a contour-style funnel plot with Egger's radial regression test (low power at k<10), with a Vevea–Hedges selection model and RoBMA available via in-browser R.

Certainty of evidence and software

Certainty in the pooled estimate is rated with GRADE across its domains, with inconsistency and imprecision updating live from the analysis. Every estimate is computed in the browser and is independently reproducible against R's metafor (a copy-paste script and an in-browser WebR run are provided).
