Did adjustment actually balance the groups?

Propensity-score matching or weighting is only as good as the balance it buys. Standardized mean differences — not significance tests — show whether treated and control groups look alike on each covariate after adjustment.

Causal · PS balance — living · live re-run illustrative demo data

What this means — in plain language

After matching or weighting, the treated and untreated groups should look alike. For each characteristic this shows whether the adjustment actually balanced them — judged on the size of the difference, not on p-values.

06

Interpretation

—

If any covariate's |SMD| stays above 0.1 after adjustment, the groups remain unbalanced on it and the effect estimate may be confounded — refine the propensity model (add interactions/splines), trim non-overlap, or adjust for the residual in the outcome model. Good balance on measured covariates says nothing about unmeasured confounding.
