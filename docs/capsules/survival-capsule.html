How much longer, not just how much less hazard

Restricted mean survival time gives a number patients understand — months of life gained up to a stated horizon — and stays valid when hazards aren't proportional, where a single HR misleads.

Type 6 · Survival / RMST — living · live re-run illustrative demo data

What this means — in plain language

Hazard ratios assume one constant effect over time, which often isn't true. This pools the restricted mean survival time — an honest summary in plain months of life gained — and checks that assumption.

06

Hazard ratio & non-PH

—

When proportional hazards is violated (e.g. delayed or crossing effects), a single HR is misleading — the RMST difference at a stated τ* is the honest summary. State τ*; pool differences, not ratios.
