How well does the test discriminate?

Sensitivity and specificity trade off against each other, so a single pooled point can mislead. A summary ROC curve shows the whole trade-off — and a threshold-effect check tells you which to trust.

What this means — in plain language

A test's sensitivity and specificity trade off against each other. This pools them across studies and draws the summary ROC curve, warning when a single 'average' accuracy would mislead.

06

Threshold, clinical translation & small-study effects

choose what to report

—

When sensitivity and the false-positive rate are strongly correlated across studies (Spearman ρ > 0.6), studies differ mainly by threshold — report the SROC curve, not a single pooled Se/Sp point.

Post-test probability (Fagan)

How the test moves a clinician's probability of disease — post-test odds = pre-test odds × LR. The solid curve is a positive result (× LR+), the dashed a negative result (× LR−); the diagonal is "no information".

Deeks' funnel — small-study effects

The DTA-appropriate publication-bias check.

Covariate meta-regression — does the modifier change accuracy?

—

Random-effects meta-regression of logit-sensitivity and logit-specificity on a study-level covariate (the Age column), each margin fit separately with a moment-estimated residual τ² and a tk−2 test. A significant slope means accuracy varies with the covariate — a source of the heterogeneity behind the SROC spread. (Margin-wise; it does not borrow the between-margin correlation that the bivariate summary uses.)

How to read this · limitations. This capsule fits the bivariate (Reitsma) random-effects model by REML maximum likelihood — the proper joint model — and draws the 95% confidence and prediction regions in-capsule (validated against mada::reitsma to < 1e-5). With few or homogeneous studies the between-study correlation can reach the ±1 boundary; it then falls back to independent margins (flagged). A strong threshold effect (Spearman ρ > 0.6) means the SROC curve, not a single point, is the honest summary. Likelihood ratios carry delta-method CIs; Deeks' funnel and its asymmetry test have low power below ~10 studies. The covariate meta-regression fits each logit margin separately (it does not borrow the between-margin correlation) and uses a tk−2 test — low power with few studies. Demo data are illustrative.
