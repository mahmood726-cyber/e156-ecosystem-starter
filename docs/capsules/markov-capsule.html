A cohort moving between health states

Most cost-effectiveness models are Markov: a cohort cycles between health states by fixed transition probabilities, accruing costs and quality-adjusted life years along the way. The whole engine is one matrix multiplied, cycle after cycle.

HTA · Markov — living · live re-run illustrative demo data

What this means — in plain language

This follows a hypothetical group of patients moving between health states over time, adding up the lifetime costs and quality-adjusted life years of competing strategies.

Cycle

One cycle = one time unit (e.g. a year). The cohort starts wholly Healthy. Costs and utilities are applied to the cohort distribution each cycle and discounted; a half-cycle correction is omitted for transparency (note it for formal models).

Assurance

    
Same self-auditing trust layer: each transition row is checked to sum to 1 (the dot turns red otherwise), the cohort trace is conserved (bands fill the full height every cycle, a visual proof the matrix is stochastic), and costs/QALYs are discounted at the stated rate. How to read this · limitations. A Markov cohort model assumes the memoryless property — transition probabilities depend only on the current state — and the result is only as good as those probabilities, the cycle length, and the discount rate. Each matrix row must sum to 1. Demo data are illustrative.
