Just above the line, just below it

When a cutoff decides who gets treated — a test score, an age, an income threshold — people just above and just below are alike except for treatment. The jump in outcome at the cutoff is a local causal effect.

Causal · RDD — living · live re-run illustrative demo data

What this means — in plain language

When a strict cutoff decides who gets treated, comparing people just above and just below it works almost like a randomised experiment. This estimates the jump in outcome right at the cutoff.

03

Assumptions

RDD is causal if units cannot precisely manipulate X around c (no sorting) and all other determinants of Y vary smoothly through c. Check a density test (no bunching at c) and covariate smoothness — not modelled here, but required.

06

Interpretation

—

The effect is local — it estimates the treatment effect only for units near the cutoff, and may not generalise. Estimates can be sensitive to bandwidth and polynomial order; vary the bandwidth here and watch the jump. This capsule uses local linear fits (no kernel weighting); production tools (rdrobust) add a triangular kernel and bias correction.
