Not worse — but worse by how much?

A non-inferiority trial doesn't ask whether the new treatment is better; it asks whether it's not unacceptably worse — cheaper, safer, simpler being the trade. The whole result hinges on one number fixed before the trial: the margin. Where the confidence interval falls relative to that margin, not relative to zero, decides everything.

What this means — in plain language

This asks not whether a new treatment is better, but whether it's not unacceptably worse — judged against a margin fixed in advance. Where the confidence interval sits relative to that margin, not relative to zero, decides everything.

01

Why the margin is everything

Δ

The pre-specified margin

OutcomeA success proportion (higher = better, e.g. cure rate). Effect = new − control risk difference. Margin ΔThe largest loss of efficacy that would still count as "not meaningfully worse" — fixed in the protocol, justified against the control's known benefit over placebo. DecisionNon-inferiority is concluded if the whole confidence interval lies above −Δ — i.e. the lower bound rules out a loss bigger than the margin. Not symmetricCrossing zero is fine; crossing −Δ is not. A wide CI sitting just above the margin still "succeeds".

A 95% two-sided CI corresponds to a one-sided test at 2.5%. The margin must be chosen before seeing the data and small enough to preserve the control's advantage over doing nothing — otherwise non-inferiority is vacuous.

05

The hidden assumption

Non-inferiority is only interpretable if the trial had assay sensitivity — the ability to have detected a difference had one existed. A sloppy trial (poor adherence, insensitive endpoint, high dropout) biases two active arms toward looking alike, making non-inferiority easier to "prove" for the wrong reason.

Intention-to-treat is conservative for superiority but anti-conservative for non-inferiority — per-protocol and ITT analyses should agree before a non-inferiority claim is trusted. A constancy assumption also applies: the margin's justification borrows from historical placebo-controlled trials whose conditions must still hold.

How to read this · limitations. A non-inferiority trial asks only whether the new treatment is not worse than the control by more than a pre-specified margin Δ — it cannot show superiority on its own, and the conclusion lives or dies by how Δ was justified. The one-sided test compares the confidence bound against −Δ. Demo data are illustrative.
