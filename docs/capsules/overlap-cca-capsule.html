The same trials, counted many times

Overviews of reviews can double-count: one primary study feeding several reviews makes the evidence look broader than it is. The corrected covered area measures that reuse, so an umbrella's "independence" can be checked, not assumed.

Meta-research · CCA — living · live re-run illustrative demo data

What this means — in plain language

When an umbrella review's reviews reuse the same underlying trials, their evidence is less independent than it looks. This measures how much of it is the same studies counted again.

Protocol

Question

InputA citation matrix: primary studies (rows) × reviews (columns), 1 if the study is included in the review. CCA(N − r) / (r·c − r), where N = filled cells, r = rows (studies), c = columns (reviews). EstimandCorrected covered area — the proportion of repeated inclusions beyond the minimum. UseQuantify overlap before pooling reviews in an overview/umbrella.

06

Interpretation

—

Pieper's CCA grades: 0–5% slight, >5–10% moderate, >10–15% high, >15% very high overlap. High overlap means reviews are not independent — pooling their conclusions over-weights the shared trials. CCA summarises the whole matrix; for pairwise detail, inspect which studies recur across which reviews above.

Assurance

    
Same self-auditing trust layer: CCA uses the corrected covered-area formula (not the uncorrected CA, which understates overlap), studies in no review are excluded from r so the denominator is honest, and the grade dot turns amber/red at Pieper's high/very-high thresholds. Accent is seeded from this capsule's slug. Tier reaches Gold only on independent reproduction.
