# Dapagliflozin in heart failure with reduced ejection fraction

Does dapagliflozin reduce cardiovascular death or worsening heart failure in adults with heart failure and reduced ejection fraction, as a hazard ratio? A multicentre randomized trial enrolled 4744 such patients across 20 countries between 2017 and 2018 with confirmed reduced ejection fraction. A Cox proportional-hazards model compared the dapagliflozin arm against placebo on the time to the first primary composite event. Dapagliflozin lowered the primary composite of cardiovascular death or worsening heart failure with a hazard ratio of 0.74 and a 95% confidence interval from 0.65 to 0.85. The benefit was consistent in patients with and without diabetes, and a prespecified subgroup analysis showed no significant interaction by baseline ejection fraction. The result indicates a clinically meaningful relative reduction in the composite endpoint, supporting dapagliflozin as add-on therapy for this population. The estimate does not extend to patients with preserved ejection fraction, who were excluded and require separate evidence.
